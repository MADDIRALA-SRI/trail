General Information and Early Life"

Full Name: Mahendra Singh Dhoni. Nicknames: He is popularly known as "Mahi", "Captain Cool", and "Thala". Date of Birth: July 7, 1981. Place of Birth: Ranchi, Bihar (now in Jharkhand), India. Early Sport: He was initially a goalkeeper for his school's football team and also played badminton. Cricket Start: His school coach, Keshav Banerjee, suggested he try wicket-keeping for the cricket team, which began his journey in cricket. Former Job: Before becoming a professional cricketer, Dhoni worked as a Travelling Ticket Examiner (TTE) at the Kharagpur Railway Station from 2001 to 2003. Family: His father's name is Pan Singh, and his mother's name is Devki Devi. He has a brother and a sister. Spouse and Daughter: He is married to Sakshi Singh Rawat (m. 2010), and they have a daughter named Ziva.

International Career & Captaincy

International Debut: He made his ODI debut on December 23, 2004, against Bangladesh, where he was run out for a duck on the first ball. Captaincy Era: He became the captain of the Indian ODI and T20 teams in 2007 and later took over Test captaincy in 2008. Only Captain with All ICC Trophies: Dhoni is the only captain in cricket history to win all three major ICC limited-overs trophies: the T20 World Cup (2007), the ODI World Cup (2011), and the Champions Trophy (2013). World Cup Wins: India won the 2011 Cricket World Cup under his leadership, the first time in 28 years. Test Ranking: Under his captaincy, India became the No. 1 Test team in the world for the first time in 2009. Retirement: He retired from Test cricket in 2014 and from international limited-overs cricket on August 15, 2020. Jersey Number: He famously wears jersey number 7, which was retired by the BCCI as a tribute to him. Honorary Rank: He holds the honorary rank of Lieutenant Colonel in the Indian Territorial Army and is a qualified paratrooper.

Batting & Wicket-keeping Records

Highest ODI Score: His highest score in ODIs is an unbeaten 183* against Sri Lanka in 2005, a world record for a wicket-keeper batsman. Highest Test Score: His top Test score is 224 against Australia in Chennai in 2013, the highest by an Indian wicket-keeper. 10,000+ ODI Runs: He is one of the few players to have scored over 10,000 ODI runs with an average of over 50. Most Stumpings: He holds the world record for the most stumpings by any wicket-keeper in international cricket across all formats. "Helicopter Shot": His signature "Helicopter Shot" was taught to him by his childhood friend, Santosh Lal.

IPL and Beyond

IPL Team: He has captained the Chennai Super Kings (CSK) for most of his IPL career, leading them to five IPL titles (2010, 2011, 2018, 2021, 2023). "Thala" Nickname: CSK fans fondly call him "Thala" (leader in Tamil). Most Expensive Player: In the inaugural IPL auction in 2008, he was the most expensive player, bought by CSK for $1.5 million. Biopic: A hit Bollywood film, M.S. Dhoni: The Untold Story, was made about his life in 2016. Other Interests: He is a known automotive enthusiast with a vast collection of bikes and cars and also co-owns a football team (Chennaiyin FC). Music Choice: He loves listening to old Hindi songs and is a big fan of Kishore Kumar
